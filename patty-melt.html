Let's Meat

November 16, 2023

Searching for an All-You-Can-Eat Korean BBQ spot in Midtown Manhattan was a surprising challenge. My Yelp results were filled with $100+ combo menus, so when I stumbled upon the $42.99 AYCE offering at Let’s Meat, I knew I had to take advantage of the rare option. Note that Let’s Meat does not allow single diners, so if you are looking to try this restaurant, plan to bring a date.

Nestled soundly in Koreatown and surrounded by more restaurants of its kind, Let’s Meat aims to keep up with the competition, enticing diners with an endless supply of meat and banchan, the only caveat being the 100 minute time limit per table.

While waiting for my first round of thin-sliced brisket and bulgogi, I snacked on a small serving on kimchi, which was promptly refilled with a much more generous portion upon proof that I wouldn’t let it go to waste. When the brisket and marinated ribeye arrived at my table, the waiter was swiftly available to toss the meat on the grill. At Let’s Meat, diners are not explicitly told to leave the cooking to the staff, but I was rarely left to monitor the meat myself. I did appreciate this style of Korean BBQ, as it elevated the experience.

The brisket was fatty and tender. I admittedly like to burn my thin sliced brisket for a slightly seared texture, and the meat held up under the excessive grill time I subjected it to. The bulgogi was equally as tender, but a little on the sweet side for my tastes. The pork belly, however, was undoubtedly the star of the show. The thick-cut strip seared skin-down on the grill developed a perfectly crisp exterior. I finished off with teriyaki chicken and another serving of brisket to complete the satisfying meal.

Let’s Meat may be a bit stingy with portions, but the quality of the meat almost compensates for it. However, it was nowhere near exceptional. It’s a solid Korean BBQ joint for the casual enjoyer, but for an avid fan like myself, I would only consider it if I was stuck in Midtown for the night and didn’t want to break the bank for it’s combo menu counterparts.

Rating: 80/100
