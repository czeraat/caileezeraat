Ikyu

January 5, 2024

Walking down 2nd Avenue on a Friday afternoon, I was not expecting to stumble on a charming, new sushi restaurant. Ikyu brands itself as “Sushi and Asian Fusion,” but while walking past those seated on my way to the back corner of the restaurant, I only noticed one table who had ordered anything other than sushi. When you first walk into Ikyu, you are immediately greeted with a stunning display of raw fish. The decor is as fresh as the food, with an airy feel, despite the lack of natural light in the tiny space.

Although I wish it had been steeped longer, the $3 pot of hot jasmine tea was massive, and I drank it like water throughout my meal. I began with the spicy toro on a wasabi-tempura cracker ($18). Each of the three pieces was loaded with toro– a more generous serving than what I am used to seeing on the Upper East Side. This dish would have been perfect, were it not for the copious amounts of eel sauce slathered on top that sometimes covered up the taste of the other elements.

I followed with a simple eel and cucumber roll ($9). The ratio of rice to filling in each piece was perfect. However, my craving for raw fish was still unsatisfied at this point, so I tacked on two pieces of salmon sashimi ($5.25/piece). What I did not expect to get for this price was the thick and soft slices of beautiful salmon in front of me. This sashimi was the highlight of my meal at Ikyu. Served with fresh wasabi, the salmon that melted in my mouth was a great way to end the meal.

With a surprisingly extensive dessert menu featuring matcha pudding and cream cheese ice cream, I will be returning to Ikyu for a chirashi bowl when I am craving some of the freshest sashimi in the city.

Rating: 90/100
