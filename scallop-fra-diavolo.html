Scallop Fra Diavolo

This dish is the recipe I make for someone when they ask me to cook a "fancy" dinner. Although the plate looks like it came from a restaurant, this pasta is quite easy to prepare.

My Scallop Fra Diavolo features sea scallops seared with butter, served over linguine tossed in a spicy tomato sauce and a splash of cream. To top it all off, I sprinkle toasted parmesan and parsley breadcrumbs for added texture.

I like to cook my pasta first beause I have found that the pasta is going to reheat in the sauce anyway, so you can just cook it and set it to the side. I sear the scallops in butter, rather than oil, because the buttery flavor adds some nice depth. Toasting the breadcrumbs for the topping adds great crunch to the dish for some fun texture.

Ingredients (makes 2 servings)

  • 1/2 pound large sea scallops
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/2 box linguine
  • olive oil
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes (adjust for spiciness level)
  • 14 oz canned whole tomatoes (half of a 28 oz can)
  • splash of heavy cream
    • for parmesan parsley breadcrumbs:
  • olive oil
  • 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup parmesan

Instructions

  1. Cook pasta according to package directions, but for one minute less. Reserve a mug full of pasta water for later.
  2. Bring a large saucepan to medium heat. While saucepan is heating, pat scallops very dry and season with salt. Add butter to pan, followed by scallops. Sear scallops on one side for just under 2 minutes, then flip and sear for another minute until a nice brown crust has developed on each side. Remove scallops from pan and set aside. Cover in foil to keep warm.
  3. In the same pan over medium heat, drizzle some olive oil. Once the oil is hot, sautee onions for 2 minutes until transluscent. Add in garlic and red pepper flakes, cooking until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  4. To the pan, add in your canned tomatoes, crushing lightly with a spoon to release the juices. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. While the sauce is cooking, bring a small saucepan to medium heat. Add breadcrumbs to pan, along with a generous amount of olive oil. Toast the breadcrumbs for a few minutes until they are golden brown, stirring constantly to prevent burning. Once they are toasted, remove them from the pan and immediately toss in parsley and parmesan.
  6. Once sauce has slightly reduced, season with salt to taste. Add in pasta, along with a few splashes of pasta water. Heat the pasta in the sauce over medium-low heat, adding more pasta water as necessary for a silky texture.
  7. Serve pasta with scallops arranged on top, sprinkling a generous amount of breadcrumbs on top. Enjoy!
