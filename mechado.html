Mechado

Mechado is a Filipino beef stew that my mom used to make me when I was younger. It is a hearty dish, served over rice. The beef gets simmered in a tomato and soy sauce based broth. Typically, the dish is made with potatoes and carrots. However, I do not like carrots so I replace them with green beans when I make it myself. My mom still insists on carrots whenever she makes it though. A squeeze of calamansi or lime juice cuts through the thickness of the broth, making for a delicious stew all year round.

I use chuck roast in this recipe because it is a fairly affordable cut of meat that tenderizes well. You can either make this dish on the stove or in pressure cooker to cut down on cooking time. On the stove, I simmer for about two hours, or until the meat is falling apart. Mechado requires a bit of planning ahead, but a lot of this recipe is downtime.

Ingredients (4 servings)

  • 3-4lbs chuck roast, cubed
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced
  • salt
  • vegetable oil
  • 2 cups water
  • 15 oz can tomato sauce
  • 1 cube beef bouillon
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 2 russet potatoes (peeled or unpeeled)
  • 2 cups green beens
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp calamansi or lime juice

Instructions

  1. Heat a large dutch oven or pot over medium heat. Pat beef dry and season with salt. Add oil to pot and sear beef in batches for 2 minutes on each side. Remove beef and set to the side.
  2. In the same pot, add onions and garlic and sautee until transluscent, about 2 minutes. Pour in water to deglaze, scraping the bottom of the pot to get all of the brown bits.
  3. Add in canned tomato sauce, beef bouillon, and soy sauce. Bring mixture to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low. Add beef back in, along with bay leaves and sugar. Let simmer for 2 hours, or until beef is tender and falling apart.
  4. Once beef is tender, add in potatoes and green beans. Cook for 15 minutes until veggies are tender.
  5. Remove from heat and stir in calamansi or lime juice. Serve over rice and enjoy!
