Mango Cake

I used to sell this mango cake for parties and events, but now that my baking business has been put on hold, I am more inclined to share this recipe. I developed this mango cake because I was wholly unsatisfied with other mango cakes on the market. From local bakeries to grocery stores, I felt as though most mango cakes lacked any real mango flavor, opting to add some slices of the fruit to a plain cake.

Where my mango cake recipe differs is the depth of flavor found throughout all the cake layers. I make a mango simple syrup to soak into the fluffy sponge cake, a mango puree for the center of the cake, using the fruit scraps from the mango syrup, and finally add fresh mango slices to the top of the cake for taste and presentation. It all gets covered with a not-too-sweet, stabilized whipped cream.

This recipe is super simple, and only requires a few ingredients you probably won't have at home. Make sure to use fresh, yellow mangos; green mangos won't have the same taste. The whipped cream can be made ahead of time and stored in the fridge because it has gelatin as a stabilizer, unlike regular whipped cream recipes.

Ingredients (9" round cake)

  • 4 eggs
  • 140g sugar
  • 90g flour
  • salt
  • 1/4 tsp baking powder
    • for mango syrup:
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 cup fresh mango
    • for mango puree:
  • 1 cup fresh mango
    • for stabilized whipped cream:
  • 2 tsp unflavored gelatin
  • 2 tbsp cold water
  • 2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup confectioners' sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Using a hand mixer or a stand mixer with a whisk attachment, whisk eggs and sugar until pale yellow in color and ribbony.
  3. Sift in flour, baking powder, and a pinch of salt. Lightly fold in this mixture, ensuring there are no flour clumps left.
  4. Divide mixture evenly into two 9" cake pans lined with parchment. Do not grease the pans. Bake for 12 minutes. Remove cakes from oven and let sit in pans for 10 minutes. Once slightly cooled, turn over onto a wire rack to let them cool completely. Slice cake layers in half horizontally to get 4 layers in total.
  5. While cakes are cooling, make mango syrup and mango puree. In a small pan, simmer water, sugar, and mango until mango has softened. Strain syrup through a fine mesh sieve, reserving mango scraps. Blend mango scraps from syrup and more mango for puree. Sweeten to taste if necessary.
  6. Make stabilized whipped cream. Put gelatin in a microwave safe bowl. Add cold water and let gelatin bloom until very thick. Microwave gelatin mixture for about 7-10 seconds until it is dissolved and liquified.
  7. Pour heavy cream into a mixing bowl that has been in the fridge for 15 minutes. Beat cream until slightly thickened. Add confectioners' sugar and vanilla and continue beating until just under the soft peak stage. While still beating, slowly pour in the liquid gelatin. Continue beating until stiff peaks form.
  8. Assemble cake! Place the first layer on your serving dish and brush over mango syrup. Spread over a thick layer of whipped cream. Add mango puree in the center. Repeat in between each layer. With the leftover whipped cream, frost the outside of the cake and decorate with fresh mango as desired. Enjoy!
