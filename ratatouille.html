Ratatouille

Watching Ratatouille as a kid, I was always so amazed with the titular dish. Served in front of food critic Ego, the saucy vegetables were so simple, yet looked extremely delicious.

When researching this dish, I discovered that ratatouille is normally served as a stew, with the vegetables diced up in sauce, quite unlike the beautiful presentation seen in the movie. The movie presentation features thinly-sliced vegetables, arranged in a repeating pattern. While the work to make this dish can be tedious, the end product is so worth it in my opinion. Plus, if you own a mandoline to slice the veggies for you, the only real work comes in when arranging them. When shopping for veggies, try to find ones that are all similar in diameter so that, when stacking them, you don't have any awkward pieces that are much larger than the others. The dish is really only a tomato sauce with veggie, so it is quite simple in flavor. However, this means that the sauce is super important to make this dish stand out, so homemade sauce is the only option!

Ratatouille is a traditionally vegetarian dish, but I have seen some variants online where meat is added to the sauce for some extra substance. I have found the dish to be suitable as main dish by itself, but I also love veggies. So, if you are inclined to think vegetables are not enough to fill you up, you may want to serve this as a side dish instead.

Ingredients

  • 2 eggplants
  • 6 roma tomatoes
  • 2 yellow squash
  • 2 zucchinis
  • olive oil
  • 1 small onion
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • salt and pepper
  • 28 oz can crushed tomatoes
  • 4 tbsp basil, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp parsley, chopped

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. Using a knife or a mandoline, thinly slice the eggplant, tomatoes, squash, and zucchini. To make assembly easy later, you can stack the veggies in an alternating pattern and set aside.
  3. Heat a cast iron skillet over medium heat. Add oil and sautee onion, garlic, and bell peppers until soft, about 10 minutes.
  4. Season to taste with salt and pepper, then add crushed tomatoes. Add in basil and simmer for about 5 minutes, until flavors have incorporated. Remove from heat.
  5. Spread the sauce in the pan into an even layer. In the pan, place your arranged veggies on top of the sauce, working to make a spiral from the edge of the pan into the center (refer to picture for arrangement). Drizzle with a generous amount of olive oil and top with salt and pepper.
  6. Cover the pan with foil and bake for 40 minutes. Uncover and bake for another 20 minutes until the veggies are soft.
  7. Top with parsley and serve. Enjoy!
